This attack chain began with a reused password and escalated into a company-wide breach affecting cloud services, email, file storage, and internal applications across multiple business divisions. Every employee in the organization was impacted, and the recovery process took weeks.

Your experience illustrates how individual security habits connect directly to organizational security. The password you reused became the attacker's way in. The app permission you almost approved would have given them persistent access. The reauthentication you completed helped close the breach. Every action you take — or do not take — matters.

---

## Lessons Learned

The Cloud Bridge scenario shows that cybersecurity is a shared responsibility. Key takeaways for every employee: use a unique, strong password for every account and manage them with a password manager, enable multi-factor authentication on all accounts and never share your MFA codes, never approve application permission requests you did not initiate or that IT has not announced, report suspicious activity immediately rather than assuming it is a false alarm, follow IT security instructions during incidents promptly and completely, and attend security awareness training — not because it is required, but because understanding threats is the best defense against them. Your vigilance is the first and most important security layer.
