You wake up Monday morning and cannot log in. The company's website is down. Your work email is unreachable. A colleague texts you: "Don't come in today. We've been hacked. Everything is encrypted."

When you finally hear from management, the news is devastating. All company systems are offline. Projects, client files, and years of work are locked behind encryption. The company may take weeks to recover — if it recovers at all. You start thinking about whether your personal information from HR systems might have been stolen as well.

---

## What Is Ransomware Deployment?

Ransomware is malicious software that locks all of your company's files and demands a payment to unlock them. Modern ransomware attackers often steal sensitive data before encrypting it, threatening to publish the data publicly if the company does not pay. When ransomware hits an entire organization at once, it means the attackers had deep access to the company's systems for days or weeks before they pulled the trigger.
